# FedRAMP 20x: Overview and Strategic Guidance

## What Is FedRAMP 20x?

FedRAMP 20x is a modernization initiative announced by the FedRAMP Program Management Office (PMO) aimed at dramatically streamlining the FedRAMP authorization process. The initiative is named to convey the goal of making authorization significantly faster and more scalable — potentially 20 times more efficient than the existing process.

### Key Changes FedRAMP 20x Introduces

**Continuous Authorization**
Rather than a point-in-time assessment leading to an ATO that periodically expires, FedRAMP 20x envisions a model of continuous monitoring and ongoing authorization. Security posture would be validated continuously through automated tooling rather than snapshot assessments.

**Automation and Machine-Readable Formats**
FedRAMP 20x emphasizes the use of OSCAL (Open Security Controls Assessment Language) — a machine-readable format for security documentation. This shifts away from large Word and PDF documents toward structured data that can be automatically validated.

**Streamlined Assessment Process**
The initiative aims to reduce the assessment burden by automating evidence collection, enabling reuse of assessment artifacts, and moving toward a more modular approach to authorization.

**Reduced Documentation Burden**
FedRAMP 20x is intended to reduce the volume of manual documentation (the traditional SSP can run 500+ pages) by replacing narrative documentation with structured, machine-readable evidence.

---

## Current Status of FedRAMP 20x

As of 2024–2025, FedRAMP 20x is in an active development and pilot phase. The FedRAMP PMO has been:
- Publishing concept papers and requests for comment
- Engaging with industry stakeholders
- Running limited pilots with select CSPs

The traditional FedRAMP authorization process (SSP, SAP, SAR, POA&M) remains the **standard required path** for all CSPs not participating in the 20x pilot.

## Should You Pursue FedRAMP 20x or Traditional Authorization?

### The Case for Traditional FedRAMP Authorization

**For most organizations in 2025, traditional authorization is the right choice:**

1. **Availability**: Traditional authorization is available to any CSP with an agency sponsor today. FedRAMP 20x is not generally available.

2. **Established process**: The traditional path has mature tooling, experienced 3PAOs, and agency reviewers who know exactly what to expect.

3. **Timeline predictability**: Traditional authorization timelines are well-understood (12–18 months for Moderate). FedRAMP 20x timelines are unknown for new entrants.

4. **Agency readiness**: Federal agencies are currently trained and equipped to review traditional FedRAMP packages. 20x packages may require additional agency-side preparation.

### The Case for FedRAMP 20x (Future Consideration)

1. **Long-term efficiency**: If FedRAMP 20x matures, continuous authorization could reduce ongoing compliance burden significantly.

2. **Modern DevSecOps alignment**: Organizations with strong automation and DevSecOps practices may find the 20x approach more natural.

3. **Reduced documentation overhead**: Less manual document authoring appeals to engineering-driven organizations.

---

## Recommendation

**Pursue traditional FedRAMP authorization now, while monitoring FedRAMP 20x development.**

### Rationale

- FedRAMP 20x is not yet generally available — waiting for it could delay your market entry by years
- The traditional path, while burdensome, delivers a real ATO that agencies will accept today
- Begin exploring OSCAL tooling in parallel, as this will be valuable regardless of which path ultimately dominates
- Revisit FedRAMP 20x participation when the PMO announces general availability

### Next Steps

1. Begin your traditional FedRAMP authorization through the Agency ATO path
2. Sign up for FedRAMP PMO newsletters and updates at fedramp.gov to monitor 20x developments
3. Start familiarizing your team with OSCAL as a future requirement
4. Consult with your 3PAO about their FedRAMP 20x readiness for future planning
