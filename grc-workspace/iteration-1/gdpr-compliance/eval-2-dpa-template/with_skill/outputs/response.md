## RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Controller wishes to use the Provider's SaaS services as described in the Main Agreement, which involves the Provider processing personal data on behalf of the Controller;

WHEREAS, the Parties intend to set out their respective obligations and rights in connection with such processing, as required by Article 28 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (the "**GDPR**");

NOW THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 27 April 2016 on the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data.

1.2 **"Personal Data"**, **"Processing"**, **"Controller"**, **"Processor"**, **"Data Subject"**, **"Supervisory Authority"**, **"Personal Data Breach"**, and **"Special Categories of Personal Data"** shall have the meanings given in Article 4 GDPR.

1.3 **"Sub-processor"** means any processor engaged by the Processor to carry out processing activities in respect of the Controller's Personal Data.

1.4 **"Data Subject Rights Request"** means any request from a Data Subject exercising rights under Articles 15–22 GDPR.

1.5 **"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means the GDPR, the UK GDPR (where applicable), and any implementing legislation in the relevant Member State(s).

---

## 2. SCOPE AND ROLES (Art. 28(3) preamble; Art. 4(7–8))

2.1 This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data carried out by the Processor on behalf of the Controller in the course of providing the SaaS services under the Main Agreement.

2.2 The Controller is the data controller as defined in Art. 4(7) GDPR. The Processor is a data processor as defined in Art. 4(8) GDPR, processing Personal Data solely on behalf of and in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions.

2.3 **Details of Processing** (as required by Art. 28(3) and Annex to this DPA):
- Subject matter, nature, and purpose: as set out in Annex 1
- Type of Personal Data: as set out in Annex 1
- Categories of Data Subjects: as set out in Annex 1
- Duration of Processing: the term of the Main Agreement plus any post-termination deletion period

---

## 3. PROCESSOR'S OBLIGATIONS — INSTRUCTIONS (Art. 28(3)(a))

3.1 The Processor shall process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, including with regard to transfers to third countries, unless required to do so by applicable EU or Member State law; in such a case, the Processor shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing, unless that law prohibits such information (Art. 28(3)(a)).

3.2 The Processor shall immediately notify the Controller if, in its opinion, an instruction infringes the GDPR or applicable data protection law (Art. 28(3) in fine).

---

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY (Art. 28(3)(b))

4.1 The Processor shall ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality (Art. 28(3)(b)).

4.2 The Processor shall limit access to Personal Data to personnel who need access on a need-to-know basis for the provision of the services.

---

## 5. SECURITY MEASURES (Art. 28(3)(c); Art. 32)

5.1 The Processor shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account:
- (a) the state of the art, costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing;
- (b) the risk of varying likelihood and severity for the rights and freedoms of natural persons (Art. 32(1)).

5.2 Such measures shall include as appropriate (Art. 32(1)(a–d)):
- (a) Pseudonymisation and encryption of Personal Data;
- (b) Ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems;
- (c) Ability to restore availability and access to Personal Data in a timely manner in the event of an incident;
- (d) A process for regularly testing, assessing, and evaluating the effectiveness of technical and organisational measures.

5.3 The Processor's current security measures are described in Annex 2.

5.4 The Processor shall evaluate risks in accordance with Art. 32(2) and shall take steps to mitigate those risks.

---

## 6. SUB-PROCESSORS (Art. 28(2); Art. 28(4))

6.1 The Processor shall not engage Sub-processors without the **prior specific or general written authorisation** of the Controller (Art. 28(2)).

6.2 Where the Controller provides general written authorisation (which it does by entering into this DPA), the Processor shall:
- (a) Notify the Controller of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors, giving the Controller the opportunity to object;
- (b) Provide at least [30] days' prior written notice of Sub-processor changes.

6.3 The list of currently authorised Sub-processors is set out in Annex 3.

6.4 Where the Processor engages Sub-processors, it shall impose the **same data protection obligations** as set out in this DPA on the Sub-processor by way of a written contract (Art. 28(4)). The Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller for the performance of the Sub-processor's obligations.

6.5 If a Sub-processor fails to fulfil its data protection obligations, the Processor shall remain fully liable to the Controller (Art. 28(4)).

---

## 7. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS ASSISTANCE (Art. 28(3)(e))

7.1 Taking into account the nature of the Processing, the Processor shall assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as possible, in fulfilling the Controller's obligation to respond to Data Subject Rights Requests under Chapter III GDPR (Arts. 15–22), including:
- Right of access (Art. 15)
- Right to rectification (Art. 16)
- Right to erasure (Art. 17)
- Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18)
- Right to data portability (Art. 20)
- Right to object (Art. 21)

7.2 The Processor shall notify the Controller of any Data Subject Rights Requests it receives without undue delay and shall not respond to such requests directly without the Controller's instruction, except to inform the Data Subject that it should contact the Controller.

---

## 8. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS ASSISTANCE (Art. 28(3)(f))

8.1 The Processor shall assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to Arts. 32–36 GDPR, taking into account the nature of processing and information available to the Processor, including:
- Security of processing (Art. 32)
- Breach notification to the supervisory authority (Art. 33)
- Communication of a breach to data subjects (Art. 34)
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (Art. 35)
- Prior consultation with supervisory authorities (Art. 36)

---

## 9. PERSONAL DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION (Art. 33; Art. 28(3)(f))

9.1 The Processor shall notify the Controller of a Personal Data Breach **without undue delay** and, where feasible, **no later than 48 hours** after becoming aware of a breach (to allow the Controller to meet its own 72-hour deadline under Art. 33(1)).

9.2 Such notification shall, to the extent possible, include (Art. 33(3)):
- (a) A description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and Personal Data records concerned;
- (b) Contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point;
- (c) Likely consequences of the breach;
- (d) Measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

9.3 Where information cannot all be provided at the same time, it may be provided in phases (Art. 33(4)).

9.4 The Processor shall maintain a breach register and cooperate with the Controller in any supervisory authority investigation.

---

## 10. DELETION AND RETURN OF DATA (Art. 28(3)(g))

10.1 At the choice of the Controller, the Processor shall, upon termination of the Main Agreement or upon request:
- (a) Delete all Personal Data and existing copies; or
- (b) Return all Personal Data to the Controller in a portable format;

unless EU or Member State law requires retention of the Personal Data (Art. 28(3)(g)).

10.2 The Processor shall confirm in writing that deletion has been completed within [30] days of the termination date.

10.3 Deletion shall include Personal Data held in backups within [90] days of the termination date, consistent with the Processor's backup retention cycle.

---

## 11. AUDIT AND INSPECTION RIGHTS (Art. 28(3)(h))

11.1 The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations in Article 28 GDPR and shall allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller (Art. 28(3)(h)).

11.2 Where a third-party auditor is used, the Controller shall give the Processor reasonable prior notice (no fewer than [30] days), ensure the auditor is subject to confidentiality obligations, and bear the costs of the audit.

11.3 The Processor may satisfy this obligation in the first instance by providing current third-party security certifications (e.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II) or audit reports. The Controller may request an on-site audit where it has reasonable grounds to believe such certifications are insufficient.

11.4 The Processor shall immediately notify the Controller if an instruction under this clause would, in the Processor's opinion, infringe the GDPR (Art. 28(3)(h) in fine).

---

## 12. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS (Art. 44–49)

12.1 The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data to a third country outside the EEA unless:
- (a) An adequacy decision applies to that third country (Art. 45); or
- (b) Appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission (Art. 46(2)(c)); or
- (c) Another approved transfer mechanism applies (Art. 46–49).

12.2 Where the Processor is established outside the EEA (e.g., in the US), the Parties shall execute the applicable EU SCCs (Controller-to-Processor modules) as Annex 4 to this DPA, or the Processor shall maintain a valid certification under the EU-US Data Privacy Framework.

12.3 The Processor shall conduct a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) where SCCs are relied upon and make this available to the Controller on request.

---

## 13. LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

13.1 Each Party shall be liable for the damage caused by Processing which infringes GDPR in accordance with Art. 82 GDPR.

13.2 The Processor is exempt from liability under Art. 82(3) if it proves it is not in any way responsible for the event giving rise to the damage.

13.3 Each Party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations set out in the Main Agreement, to the extent permitted by applicable law.

---

## 14. TERM AND TERMINATION

14.1 This DPA shall remain in effect for the duration of the Main Agreement and shall automatically terminate upon termination or expiry of the Main Agreement.

14.2 Obligations under Sections 10 (data deletion) and 11 (audit) shall survive termination.
