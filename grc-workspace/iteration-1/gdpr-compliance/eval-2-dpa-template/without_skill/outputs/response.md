## 1. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this DPA:

- **"GDPR"** means the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679.
- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
- **"Processing"** means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data.
- **"Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data.
- **"Processor"** means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of a Controller.
- **"Sub-processor"** means any third party engaged by the Processor to process Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.
- **"Data Subject"** means the natural person to whom Personal Data relates.
- **"Personal Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to Personal Data.

## 3. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Processing on Instructions Only

The Processor shall process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, as set out in this DPA and the Main Agreement. The Processor shall inform the Controller promptly if it believes any instruction infringes applicable data protection law.

### 3.2 Confidentiality

The Processor shall ensure that all personnel authorised to process Personal Data are bound by an appropriate obligation of confidentiality and are trained on data protection requirements.

### 3.3 Security

The Processor shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk of the Processing, including:

- Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest
- Measures to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability, and resilience of processing systems
- The ability to restore access to Personal Data in a timely manner following a technical incident
- A process for regularly testing and evaluating the effectiveness of security measures

A description of the security measures currently in place is set out in Schedule 2.

### 3.4 Sub-processors

- The Processor shall not engage any Sub-processor without the prior written consent of the Controller (which may be given generally in advance through this DPA).
- The Processor shall maintain an up-to-date list of Sub-processors and provide advance notice (at least 30 days) of any changes, giving the Controller opportunity to object.
- The Processor shall impose equivalent data protection obligations on all Sub-processors through a written contract.
- The Processor remains liable for the acts and omissions of its Sub-processors.
- Current Sub-processors are listed in Schedule 3.

### 3.5 Assistance with Data Subject Rights

The Processor shall, insofar as possible, assist the Controller in responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under the GDPR, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

The Processor shall promptly forward to the Controller any Data Subject request it receives and shall not respond to such requests directly without the Controller's authorisation.

### 3.6 Assistance with Controller Obligations

The Processor shall provide reasonable assistance to the Controller in:
- Complying with its security obligations
- Notifying data breaches to the supervisory authority and affected individuals
- Conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
- Prior consultation with supervisory authorities

### 3.7 Breach Notification

The Processor shall notify the Controller of any Personal Data Breach without undue delay and in any event within 48 hours of becoming aware of the breach. The notification shall include:
- A description of the nature of the breach
- Contact details for the data protection contact
- Likely consequences of the breach
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach

### 3.8 Deletion and Return of Data

Upon termination of the Main Agreement or upon the Controller's request, the Processor shall, at the Controller's choice:
- Return all Personal Data to the Controller; or
- Securely delete all Personal Data and confirm deletion in writing within 30 days

unless applicable law requires the Processor to retain the data.

### 3.9 Audit and Inspection Rights

The Processor shall make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and shall allow for and cooperate with audits conducted by the Controller or its appointed auditors. The Processor may satisfy this requirement by providing current third-party audit reports (such as SOC 2 or ISO 27001 certifications).
