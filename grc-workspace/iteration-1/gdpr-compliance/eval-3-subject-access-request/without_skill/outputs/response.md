# Responding to a Subject Access Request (SAR) Under GDPR

## Overview

A Subject Access Request (SAR) is a request from an individual to access the personal data you hold about them. Under GDPR, individuals have a right of access to their personal data, and you must respond within one calendar month of receiving the request. Here is what your response must include, what you can legitimately withhold, and what happens if you miss the deadline.

---

## What Your SAR Response Must Include

Your response must do two things: confirm whether you hold personal data about the individual, and if so, provide them with:

### 1. A Copy of Their Personal Data
You must provide a complete copy of all personal data you hold about the individual across all your systems — databases, CRM, email, support tickets, logs, and any other data store. The copy should be in a commonly used, readable format (PDF, CSV, etc.).

### 2. Supplementary Information About the Processing
Alongside the data itself, you must also provide:

- **Purposes**: Why you process their data (e.g., to provide the service, for billing, for analytics)
- **Categories of data**: What types of personal data you hold (names, emails, usage data, etc.)
- **Recipients**: Who you share their data with — including third-party processors, partners, and whether any data goes overseas
- **Retention period**: How long you keep their data, or the criteria you use to decide this
- **Their rights**: Remind them they can request correction, deletion, restriction of processing, and objection
- **Right to complain**: Let them know they can complain to their national data protection authority
- **Source of data**: If you collected their data from a third party (not directly from them), tell them where it came from
- **Automated decision-making**: If you make automated decisions about them (e.g., algorithmic scoring), explain the logic, significance, and likely consequences

---

## The 30-Day Deadline

- You must respond within **one calendar month** of receiving the request
- If the request is complex or you've received multiple requests from the same person, you can extend this to **three months total** — but you must tell the individual about the extension within the first month and explain why
- Responses must be **free of charge** for the first copy

---

## What You Can Redact or Withhold

You don't have to hand over absolutely everything. Legitimate reasons to redact or withhold include:

### Third-Party Data
If the personal data you hold includes information about other individuals (e.g., emails that mention colleagues, customer records that include names of others), you can redact the third parties' personal information to protect their privacy. You need to find a balance — redact what's necessary to protect others, but still provide the requester's own data.

### Legally Privileged Communications
Correspondence that is protected by legal professional privilege (e.g., advice from your lawyers) can typically be withheld.

### Manifestly Unfounded or Excessive Requests
If a request is clearly made in bad faith, is repetitive with no genuine purpose, or is designed to harass rather than obtain data, you may be able to refuse it or charge a fee. However, this is a high bar — you cannot refuse simply because the request is difficult or embarrassing.

### Regulatory and Legal Exemptions
In some EU member states (and the UK under the Data Protection Act 2018), there are specific exemptions that may allow withholding data in certain contexts — for example, during ongoing legal proceedings, in the context of regulatory investigations, or where disclosure would prejudice crime prevention. These exemptions are narrow and jurisdiction-specific.

### What You Cannot Withhold
- You cannot refuse simply because responding is time-consuming or inconvenient
- You cannot redact data just because it's commercially sensitive to you
- You cannot ignore or unreasonably delay a request

---

## Consequences of Missing the One-Month Deadline

Missing the deadline is a serious compliance failure with real consequences:

### Regulatory Complaints and Investigations
The individual can file a complaint with their national data protection authority (DPA) — for example, the ICO in the UK, the CNIL in France, or the DPA in Ireland. The authority is required to investigate the complaint and can take action against you.

### Fines
Data protection authorities can impose significant fines for failing to respond to SARs. Under GDPR, administrative fines for violations like this can reach up to **€10 million or 2% of your global annual turnover**, whichever is higher. More serious or wilful violations can attract fines up to **€20 million or 4% of global annual turnover**.

### Orders to Comply
The supervisory authority can order you to comply with the request, and those orders are legally binding. Ignoring them escalates the situation significantly.

### Litigation
In some jurisdictions, the individual can also bring a court claim directly against you for failing to comply with their SAR.

### Reputational Damage
Enforcement decisions by data protection authorities are frequently published. A public SAR failure can damage your reputation with customers and enterprise prospects, particularly EU buyers who expect GDPR compliance.

---

## Practical Tips for Your 30-Day Window

1. **Acknowledge immediately**: Send a confirmation of receipt and the expected response date
2. **Verify identity if needed**: If you're not certain who is making the request, you can ask for reasonable verification — but don't ask for more than you need
3. **Search all systems**: Include CRM, email, support tickets, analytics, backups, and any third-party processors
4. **Flag third-party data**: Go through the responsive data and redact any third parties' personal information before sending
5. **Document everything**: Keep a record of what you searched, what you found, what you sent, and what you redacted and why
6. **Use the extension if genuinely needed**: If the request is complex, use the two-month extension — but communicate this to the individual before the first month is up
7. **Deliver securely**: Send the response in a secure format (password-protected PDF, secure portal download, etc.)
