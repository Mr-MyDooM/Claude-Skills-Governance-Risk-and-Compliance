## ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(1); 45 CFR §160.103

**[REQUIRED]**

1.1 **"Breach"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §164.402: the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of such information, subject to the exclusions set forth therein.

1.2 **"Business Associate"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §160.103.

1.3 **"Covered Entity"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §160.103.

1.4 **"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media, as defined at 45 CFR §160.103.

1.5 **"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §160.103, limited to the PHI created, received, maintained, or transmitted by the Business Associate on behalf of the Covered Party.

1.6 **"Required by Law"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §164.103.

1.7 **"Secretary"** means the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) or his/her designee.

1.8 **"Security Incident"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §164.304.

1.9 **"Subcontractor"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §160.103.

1.10 **"Unsecured PHI"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR §164.402.

All other capitalized terms not defined herein shall have the meanings ascribed to them under HIPAA.

## ARTICLE 2 — OBLIGATIONS AND ACTIVITIES OF THE BUSINESS ASSOCIATE
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)

**[REQUIRED]**

2.1 **Permitted Uses and Disclosures.** The Business Associate may use or disclose PHI only:
   (a) As necessary to perform the Services described in the underlying services agreement between the Parties ("Services Agreement");
   (b) As Required by Law; or
   (c) As otherwise permitted or required by this Agreement.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(i)

2.2 **Prohibition on Unauthorized Use or Disclosure.** The Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required by Law.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(A)

2.3 **Safeguards.** The Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI that it creates, receives, maintains, or transmits on behalf of the Covered Party, in accordance with 45 CFR §§164.308, 164.310, and 164.312 (the HIPAA Security Rule).
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(B); 45 CFR §164.306

2.4 **Minimum Necessary.** The Business Associate shall, to the extent practicable, request and use only the minimum necessary PHI to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request.
// 45 CFR §164.514(d)

2.5 **Reporting of Breaches and Security Incidents.**
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(C); 45 CFR §164.410

   (a) **Breach Notification.** The Business Associate shall notify the Covered Party of any Breach of Unsecured PHI without unreasonable delay and in no case later than **60 calendar days** after the Business Associate discovers such Breach. Such notification shall include, to the extent possible:
      - The identification of each individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed;
      - A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery;
      - A description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved;
      - Any steps individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm;
      - A brief description of what the Business Associate is doing to investigate, mitigate harm, and prevent future occurrences.

   (b) **Security Incidents.** The Business Associate shall report to the Covered Party any Security Incident of which it becomes aware, including attempted and successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI.

   **[RECOMMENDED]** The Parties agree that the Business Associate's obligation to report Security Incidents is satisfied by the Business Associate providing notice of the categories of Security Incidents on a **[quarterly / monthly]** basis, except that actual Breaches must be reported individually pursuant to Section 2.5(a).

2.6 **Subcontractors.** The Business Associate shall ensure that any Subcontractor that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of the Business Associate agrees to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to the Business Associate under this Agreement by executing a written agreement (a subcontractor BAA) meeting the requirements of 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2) and 45 CFR §164.308(b)(2) before disclosing PHI to such Subcontractor.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(D)

2.7 **Access to PHI.** The Business Associate shall make available PHI in a designated record set to the Covered Party or, at the Covered Party's direction, to an individual, within **30 days** of a request, in a manner that permits the Covered Party to fulfill its obligations under 45 CFR §164.524.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(E)

2.8 **Amendment of PHI.** The Business Associate shall make PHI available for amendment and incorporate any amendments to PHI in a designated record set upon request by the Covered Party, consistent with 45 CFR §164.526.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(F)

2.9 **Accounting of Disclosures.** The Business Associate shall maintain documentation of all disclosures of PHI and information related to such disclosures necessary for the Covered Party to respond to a request for an accounting of disclosures pursuant to 45 CFR §164.528. The Business Associate shall provide such documentation to the Covered Party within **30 days** of a request.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(G)

2.10 **Access by HHS.** The Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI received from the Covered Party available to the Secretary for purposes of determining the Covered Party's and Business Associate's compliance with HIPAA.
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(ii)(H)

2.11 **Policies and Procedures.** The Business Associate shall implement and maintain written policies and procedures for each of the standards and implementation specifications required under the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR §164.316(a)).

2.12 **Training.** The Business Associate shall train all members of its workforce who have access to PHI on HIPAA requirements and the Business Associate's HIPAA policies and procedures, consistent with 45 CFR §164.530(b).

## ARTICLE 3 — PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES BY BUSINESS ASSOCIATE
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(3)-(4)

**[REQUIRED]**

3.1 **Services Performance.** The Business Associate is authorized to use PHI for the proper management and administration of the Business Associate's services to the Covered Party.

3.2 **Management and Administration.** The Business Associate may use PHI for the Business Associate's own management and administration and legal responsibilities, provided that:
   (a) Such disclosures are Required by Law; or
   (b) The Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances in writing from the person to whom the information is disclosed that it will be held confidentially, used or further disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and the person will notify the Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been breached.

3.3 **Data Aggregation.** **[OPTIONAL — include only if the BA will perform data aggregation services]** The Business Associate may use PHI to provide data aggregation services to the Covered Party as permitted by 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(i)(B).

3.4 **De-Identification.** The Business Associate may de-identify PHI in accordance with 45 CFR §164.514(a)-(c). Once de-identified, such data is no longer PHI and is not subject to this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 4 — OBLIGATIONS OF THE COVERED PARTY

4.1 **Notice of Privacy Practices.** The Covered Party shall notify the Business Associate of any limitations in its Notice of Privacy Practices pursuant to 45 CFR §164.520, to the extent such limitations may affect the Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

4.2 **Permissions and Restrictions.** The Covered Party shall notify the Business Associate of:
   (a) Any changes in, or revocation of, the permission by an individual to use or disclose his or her PHI; and
   (b) Any restrictions to the use or disclosure of PHI that the Covered Party has agreed to in accordance with 45 CFR §164.522.

4.3 **Permissible Requests.** The Covered Party shall not request the Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under HIPAA if done by the Covered Party.

## ARTICLE 5 — TERM AND TERMINATION
// 45 CFR §164.504(e)(2)(iii)

**[REQUIRED]**

5.1 **Term.** This Agreement shall be effective as of the Effective Date and shall remain in effect until terminated by either Party or until all PHI provided by the Covered Party to the Business Associate is destroyed or returned to the Covered Party.

5.2 **Termination for Cause.** Upon the Covered Party's knowledge of a material breach of this Agreement by the Business Associate, the Covered Party shall:
   (a) Provide the Business Associate with an opportunity to cure the breach or end the violation; if the Business Associate fails to cure the breach within **30 days** of receiving notice, the Covered Party may terminate this Agreement; or
   (b) Immediately terminate this Agreement if cure is not possible.

5.3 **Effect of Termination.**

   (a) **Return or Destruction of PHI.** Upon termination, the Business Associate shall, at the Covered Party's election, either return or destroy all PHI received from, or created or received by the Business Associate on behalf of, the Covered Party. The Business Associate shall not retain any copies of the PHI.

   (b) **Infeasibility of Return or Destruction.** If return or destruction of PHI is not feasible, the Business Associate shall:
      - Notify the Covered Party in writing with an explanation of why return or destruction is infeasible;
      - Extend the protections of this Agreement to the retained PHI; and
      - Limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible.
      
   **[RECOMMENDED]** Return or destruction shall be certified in writing by the Business Associate within **30 days** of termination.

5.4 **Survival.** The obligations of the Business Associate under this Article shall survive the termination of this Agreement.
