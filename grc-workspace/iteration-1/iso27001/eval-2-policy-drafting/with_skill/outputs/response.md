## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy establishes the overarching framework for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of [Company Name]'s information assets, systems, and the data entrusted to us by our customers. It demonstrates management commitment to information security and provides direction for all supporting policies, standards, and procedures.

This policy supports [Company Name]'s Information Security Management System (ISMS) established in accordance with **ISO/IEC 27001:2022**.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- **All employees**, contractors, consultants, temporary workers, and third-party personnel with access to [Company Name] systems or information
- **All information assets**, including data, software, hardware, and services regardless of format (electronic, physical, or verbal)
- **All systems and environments**, including production, staging, development, corporate IT, and cloud-hosted services
- **All locations**, including company offices, remote/home working environments, and third-party facilities

This policy covers all activities related to the creation, storage, transmission, processing, and disposal of [Company Name] information.

---

## 3. Policy Statement

[Company Name]'s management is committed to preserving the **confidentiality**, **integrity**, and **availability** of all information assets in support of our business objectives, customer obligations, and applicable legal and regulatory requirements.

[Company Name] will:

1. **Establish and maintain an ISMS** aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 to systematically manage information security risks
2. **Identify and assess information security risks** and implement appropriate controls to reduce risks to an acceptable level
3. **Comply with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations** relating to information security, privacy, and data protection (including relevant data protection legislation)
4. **Protect customer data**, including personally identifiable information (PII) and financial data, with the highest priority and care
5. **Provide information security awareness and training** to all personnel to ensure they understand their responsibilities
6. **Respond effectively to information security incidents** to minimise business impact and prevent recurrence
7. **Continually improve** our ISMS through regular reviews, audits, and management oversight
8. **Hold all personnel accountable** for the security of information they access, process, or handle

## 6. Information Security Objectives

[Company Name] establishes measurable information security objectives aligned with this policy. These objectives are reviewed annually during management review (Clause 9.3) and include, but are not limited to:

- Maintain zero critical unpatched vulnerabilities in production systems beyond agreed SLA
- Achieve and maintain 100% employee completion of annual security awareness training
- Respond to all high-severity security incidents within defined SLA targets
- Complete annual risk assessment and update risk register
- Complete annual internal ISMS audit
- Maintain ISO 27001:2022 certification

Objectives are tracked and reported on a quarterly basis by the CISO.

---

## 7. Risk Management

[Company Name] will maintain a formal risk assessment and treatment process in accordance with Clause 6.1 of ISO 27001:2022. Risks to the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets will be:

- Identified and assessed using a documented methodology (likelihood × impact)
- Treated through mitigation, acceptance, transfer, or avoidance
- Tracked in a Risk Register reviewed at least annually
- Reflected in a Statement of Applicability (SoA) that documents which Annex A controls are applicable

---

## 8. Compliance

### Legal and Regulatory
[Company Name] will identify and comply with all applicable information security legislation and regulations, including:
- Data protection and privacy laws applicable to jurisdictions where we operate and process customer data
- Payment card industry requirements where applicable
- Contractual obligations with customers, partners, and suppliers

### Policy Compliance
All personnel are required to comply with this policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract. Serious violations may be referred to law enforcement authorities.

The CISO will conduct periodic compliance reviews and report findings to executive management.

---

## 9. Incident Reporting

All personnel must **immediately report** any actual or suspected information security incident to the Security team at [security@company.com] or via the incident reporting portal. This includes:

- Suspected data breaches or unauthorised access
- Loss or theft of devices containing company data
- Malware or phishing attempts
- Accidental sharing of confidential information

Failure to report a suspected security incident is itself a policy violation.

---

## 10. Review and Maintenance

This policy will be reviewed:
- **Annually** as part of the management review process (Clause 9.3)
- Following any significant security incident
- Following significant changes to [Company Name]'s business, technology environment, or regulatory context
- At the direction of the CEO or CISO

All revisions require approval by the CEO before publication.
