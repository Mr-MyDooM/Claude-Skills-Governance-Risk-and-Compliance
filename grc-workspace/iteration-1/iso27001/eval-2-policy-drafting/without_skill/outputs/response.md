## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Information Security Policy establishes [Company Name]'s commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets. It applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who access our systems or data.

---

## 2. Our Commitment

[Company Name] is committed to:

- Protecting the information assets of the company and our customers
- Complying with all applicable laws and regulations related to information security and privacy
- Implementing controls proportionate to the risks we face as a fintech company handling sensitive financial data
- Maintaining and continually improving our Information Security Management System (ISMS)
- Providing employees with the training and resources they need to keep information secure

---

## 3. Key Principles

### 3.1 Confidentiality
Information will only be accessible to those who are authorised to access it. Access to sensitive customer data and financial information is granted on a need-to-know basis.

### 3.2 Integrity
Information will be accurate and protected from unauthorised modification. Systems and data will be maintained in a reliable state.

### 3.3 Availability
Information and systems will be available to authorised users when needed, with appropriate backup and recovery measures in place.

---

## 4. Responsibilities

**Executive Management** is responsible for:
- Approving and supporting this policy
- Allocating appropriate resources for information security
- Setting the tone for a security-aware culture

**The Security/IT Team** is responsible for:
- Implementing and maintaining technical security controls
- Managing access rights and monitoring systems
- Responding to security incidents
- Conducting risk assessments

**All Employees** are responsible for:
- Following this policy and all related security procedures
- Completing required security awareness training
- Reporting security incidents or concerns immediately
- Protecting the devices and accounts they use to access company systems

**Managers** are responsible for:
- Ensuring their teams understand and comply with security requirements
- Participating in security risk discussions relevant to their areas

---

## 5. Core Security Requirements

### Access Control
- All systems must require strong authentication; multi-factor authentication (MFA) is required for all production systems and applications handling customer data
- Access is granted based on least privilege and business need
- Access rights are reviewed quarterly and revoked promptly upon employee departure

### Data Protection
- Customer PII and financial data must be encrypted in transit and at rest
- Data must be classified and handled according to its sensitivity
- Data is retained only as long as necessary and disposed of securely

### Device and Endpoint Security
- Company devices must have current anti-malware software installed
- All devices must use full-disk encryption
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately

### Software and Systems
- Systems must be kept up to date with security patches
- Only approved software may be installed on company devices
- Development must follow secure coding practices

### Third Parties and Suppliers
- All third parties with access to company data must meet our minimum security requirements
- Contracts with suppliers must include appropriate security obligations

### Incident Management
- All suspected security incidents must be reported immediately to the security team
- A formal incident response process is maintained and tested regularly

---

## 6. Risk Management

[Company Name] maintains a formal process for identifying and managing information security risks. Risks are assessed, prioritised, and treated appropriately. A risk register is maintained and reviewed at least annually.

---

## 7. Compliance

All employees must comply with this policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination. Serious violations may be reported to relevant authorities.

Compliance with this policy and related controls is monitored through:
- Regular internal audits
- Management reviews
- Security monitoring and alerting
