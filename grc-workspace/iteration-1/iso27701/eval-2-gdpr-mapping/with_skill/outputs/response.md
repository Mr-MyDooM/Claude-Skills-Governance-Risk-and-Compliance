# ISO 27701 to GDPR Mapping

## Overview

ISO 27701:2025 includes an updated GDPR correspondence annex (equivalent to Annex D in the 2019 edition) that maps standard controls directly to GDPR articles and recitals. This mapping is one of the most strategically valuable aspects of ISO 27701 certification for organizations subject to the EU General Data Protection Regulation.

The key principle: **ISO 27701 certification does not equal GDPR compliance**, but it provides a structured, independently audited framework that addresses the vast majority of GDPR obligations. A certified organization can present its Statement of Applicability (SoA) and audit report as substantive evidence of a systematic approach to privacy management.

## How ISO 27701 Certification Helps with GDPR Compliance

### What Certification Demonstrates

1. **Systematic approach**: Certification by an accredited third-party body demonstrates that your privacy management is not ad hoc but operates within a documented, audited management system.

2. **Technical and Organisational Measures (TOMs)**: Article 24 GDPR requires controllers to implement "appropriate technical and organisational measures." Your ISO 27701 SoA and certification report constitute documented evidence of those measures.

3. **Accountability principle (Article 5(2))**: The GDPR requires controllers to be able to demonstrate compliance — not just claim it. A certified PIMS with maintained records (RoPA, DPIA records, DSR handling records, audit reports) directly satisfies this accountability requirement.

4. **Due diligence for processors (Article 28)**: When selecting processors, controllers must choose those providing "sufficient guarantees" of appropriate technical and organisational measures. ISO 27701 processor certification is widely accepted as evidence of those guarantees, simplifying vendor due diligence.

5. **Supervisory authority engagement**: The UK ICO explicitly recognises ISO 27701 certification as a meaningful privacy standard. Several EU supervisory authorities have acknowledged it as a relevant compliance indicator, though it does not function as a formal GDPR certification scheme under Article 42.

### Important Limitations

- **ISO 27701 is not an Article 42 GDPR certification scheme.** Under GDPR Article 42, supervisory authorities (via EDPB) can approve certification schemes that confer formal compliance presumptions. ISO 27701 is not (yet) approved as such a scheme in most EU jurisdictions.
- **Certification covers the system, not every decision.** An ISO 27701 audit verifies your management system operates correctly — it does not guarantee that every individual processing decision (e.g., every specific consent capture) is GDPR-compliant.
- **Lawful basis is a legal determination.** Auditors verify you have documented lawful bases, but determining whether a specific lawful basis is legally sound for a given processing activity remains a legal analysis.

## Summary: Coverage Assessment

| GDPR Chapter | Coverage Level | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Chapter I — General provisions | High | Principles mapped directly |
| Chapter II — Lawfulness (Arts 5–11) | High | Lawful basis, consent, special categories |
| Chapter III — Data subject rights (Arts 12–23) | Very High | All 8 rights have dedicated controls (A.1.3.5–A.1.3.11) |
| Chapter IV — Controller/processor (Arts 24–43) | Very High | DPO, DPbD, DPIAs, processor contracts, RoPA, breach |
| Chapter V — Transfers (Arts 44–49) | High | Transfer mechanisms documented per transfer |
| Chapter VI — Supervisory authorities | Partial | Cooperation supported but not directly controlled |
| Chapter VIII — Remedies | Partial | Incident response and breach notification covered |
