# ISO 27701 and GDPR: Mapping and Compliance Benefits

## Overview

ISO 27701 was designed with explicit attention to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and includes a correspondence annex (Annex D in the 2019 edition) that maps standard controls to GDPR articles. This alignment makes ISO 27701 one of the most GDPR-relevant privacy certifications available to organizations.

However, it is important to understand upfront: **ISO 27701 certification is not equivalent to GDPR compliance**, but it provides strong, independently audited evidence of a structured privacy management approach that addresses the majority of GDPR obligations.

---

## How ISO 27701 Maps to GDPR Principles (Article 5)

The six GDPR data processing principles in Article 5 are directly addressed by ISO 27701 controls:

| GDPR Principle | GDPR Article | ISO 27701 Coverage |
|----------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Lawfulness, fairness, transparency | Art. 5(1)(a) | Privacy notice controls, lawful basis documentation, consent management |
| Purpose limitation | Art. 5(1)(b) | Records of processing activities (RoPA), purpose specification controls |
| Data minimisation | Art. 5(1)(c) | Privacy by design controls |
| Accuracy | Art. 5(1)(d) | Data subject rectification right procedure |
| Storage limitation | Art. 5(1)(e) | Retention policies, disposal procedures |
| Integrity and confidentiality | Art. 5(1)(f) | Security controls (encryption, access control, incident response) |
| Accountability | Art. 5(2) | Management system documentation, SoA, audit records |

## Key GDPR Article Mappings

### Lawful Basis (Articles 6–9)

ISO 27701 requires organizations to document the lawful basis for every processing activity in their Records of Processing Activities. It also includes controls for consent management — capturing consent in a format that can be demonstrated, recorded, and withdrawn.

For special categories of personal data (Article 9), ISO 27701 requires identification of these data types and enhanced controls, including Data Protection Impact Assessments for high-risk processing.

### Data Subject Rights (Articles 15–22)

This is one of ISO 27701's strongest GDPR alignment areas. The standard includes specific controls for each of the eight GDPR data subject rights:

- **Right of access** (Art. 15): Procedure for responding to subject access requests
- **Right to rectification** (Art. 16): Process for correcting inaccurate data
- **Right to erasure** (Art. 17): "Right to be forgotten" procedure
- **Right to restriction** (Art. 18): Procedure for restricting processing
- **Right to data portability** (Art. 20): Data export in machine-readable format
- **Right to object** (Art. 21): Handling objections to processing
- **Rights related to automated decision-making** (Art. 22): Controls for profiling and automated decisions

### Transparency (Articles 12–14)

ISO 27701 includes controls for privacy notices — both for data collected directly from individuals and data obtained from third parties — mapping to the transparency information requirements in GDPR Articles 13 and 14.

### Controller and Processor Obligations (Articles 24–28)

ISO 27701 explicitly distinguishes between PII controller and PII processor obligations:

- **Controllers** must implement privacy by design and by default, maintain a RoPA, conduct DPIAs for high-risk processing, and manage processor contracts.
- **Processors** must operate only on controller instructions, maintain their own RoPA, manage sub-processors, assist controllers with data subject rights, and cooperate with audits.

This maps directly to GDPR Articles 24–28 obligations.

### Records of Processing Activities (Article 30)

ISO 27701's RoPA requirements align closely with GDPR Article 30. The RoPA must document processing purposes, data categories, data subject categories, recipients, retention periods, and transfer mechanisms — identical to Article 30 requirements.

### Security (Article 32)

ISO 27701's security controls address the Article 32 requirement for "appropriate technical and organisational measures" including pseudonymisation, encryption, availability, resilience, and regular security testing.

### Breach Notification (Articles 33–34)

ISO 27701 includes privacy incident response controls covering detection, assessment, and notification of data breaches — including the 72-hour supervisory authority notification timeline and the requirement to notify affected individuals where there is high risk.

### Data Protection Impact Assessments (Article 35)

ISO 27701 includes a DPIA control with trigger criteria matching GDPR Article 35 — systematic profiling, processing of special categories at scale, systematic monitoring, new technologies. The standard requires documented DPIA results and procedures for prior consultation with supervisory authorities where high residual risk remains.

### Data Protection Officer (Articles 37–39)

ISO 27701 Clause 5 (Leadership) addresses the appointment of a DPO or privacy officer with documented responsibilities, authority, and independence — matching GDPR DPO requirements.

### International Data Transfers (Articles 44–49)

ISO 27701 requires transfer mechanisms to be documented for every transfer to third countries — Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, Binding Corporate Rules — mapped per processing activity in the RoPA.

## How Certification Helps with GDPR Compliance

### Demonstrating Technical and Organisational Measures

GDPR Article 24 requires controllers to implement "appropriate technical and organisational measures." An ISO 27701 certification — issued by an accredited certification body after a formal audit — constitutes strong documented evidence of these measures.

### Satisfying the Accountability Principle

GDPR's accountability principle (Article 5(2)) requires controllers to be able to demonstrate compliance. The management system documentation required by ISO 27701 — policies, risk assessments, training records, audit reports, DSR handling logs — creates exactly the accountability documentation trail the GDPR demands.

### Vendor Due Diligence

Under GDPR Article 28, controllers must only engage processors providing "sufficient guarantees" of appropriate measures. An ISO 27701-certified processor can provide their certification and SoA as evidence, significantly streamlining customer due diligence and contract negotiations.

### Regulator Engagement

Several EU data protection authorities recognise ISO 27701 as a meaningful privacy standard. The UK ICO has explicitly acknowledged its relevance. While it is not a formal GDPR certification scheme under Article 42, it is widely viewed by regulators as a positive indicator of privacy maturity.

---

## Important Limitations

1. **Not an Article 42 GDPR certification**: GDPR Article 42 allows supervisory authorities to approve formal certification schemes that confer legal presumptions of compliance. ISO 27701 is not currently approved as such a scheme in most EU member states — meaning certification does not provide a formal legal safe harbor.

2. **System certification, not decision certification**: Auditors verify that your privacy management system works correctly as a system — not that every individual decision (e.g., every consent capture, every data subject request response) is legally correct.

3. **Legal analysis still required**: Determining whether specific processing activities are lawful under GDPR requires legal judgment. ISO 27701 helps structure and document that analysis but does not replace it.

4. **Scope matters**: Certification only covers the scope defined in your PIMS. Processing activities outside scope are not covered.

---

## Summary

ISO 27701 provides one of the most comprehensive, internationally recognised mappings to GDPR of any privacy standard available. Achieving certification demonstrates a systematic, audited approach to privacy management that addresses Articles 5–49 across the GDPR's key requirements. While it does not confer formal GDPR compliance status, it significantly strengthens your compliance position, simplifies vendor due diligence, and provides robust documentation for regulatory engagement.
